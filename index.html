Game Rules (Rules 1.0–11.0)
1.0All games governed by SPN rules except where HCCSL rules specifically override.
2.0Teams must have a minimum of 8 players (including at least 3 women) to start a game. Fewer than 8 = forfeit.
2.1Maximum of 10 players on the field. Batting order may include up to 16 players.
3.0Gender ratio: At least 3 women in the field at all times. Violation = automatic out each inning.
4.0Games are 7 innings or 75 minutes, whichever comes first. No new inning starts after the time limit.
4.1Mercy rule: 15 runs after 3 innings, 12 runs after 4, 10 runs after 5.
5.0Pitching: Arc pitch — minimum 6 ft, maximum 12 ft. Pitcher must stay behind the rubber until the ball is hit.
5.1Batter starts with a 1-1 count. Foul on 2-strike count = out.
6.0Home run limit: 3 HR per team per game. Additional HRs = out.
6.1Unlimited encroachment HRs (when opposing team has more HRs) do not count against the limit.
7.0Base running: No leading or stealing. Runner may leave base when the ball crosses home plate.
7.1Courtesy runner allowed once per inning. Runner must be the last recorded out.
8.0Weather cancellations: Game called before 4 innings = no game. 4+ innings complete = official result. Rainout = 7–7 tie for standings purposes.
9.0Tie games: Remain as ties in the standings (1 point each). No extra innings in regular season.
10.0Standings tiebreakers: a) Head-to-head points · b) Last matchup result · c) Coin toss.
11.0Run differential capped at +7 per game for standings purposes.
Player Eligibility (Rules 12.0–15.0)
12.0Players must be registered with SPN and on the official team roster before playing.
12.1Minimum age: 18 years by the first game of the season.
13.0Pickup players: Permitted to avoid forfeit. Max 2 pickups per game. Pickups bat last.
13.1Pickup players are not eligible for playoffs.
14.0Players may only be on one team roster per season.
15.0Roster changes (additions/drops) must be submitted to the convenor before the player's first game.
Conduct & Sportsmanship (Rules 16.0–19.0)
16.0Unsportsmanlike conduct, arguing with umpires, or threatening behaviour = ejection.
16.1Ejected players must leave the field and surrounding area. Team plays short.
17.0Fighting = automatic suspension. Duration determined by the convenor.
17.1Team plays short.
18.0Alcohol on the diamond or dugout = ejection. Permitted in designated areas only.
18.1Coaches responsible for spectator conduct. Failure to control spectators can result in ejection or forfeit.
19.0Games must be officiated by at least 1 umpire. No umpire = game cancelled.
